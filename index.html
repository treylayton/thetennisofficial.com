Four players. Two opinions. Zero umpire.

Recreational tennis has no chair umpire. When a close call happens, the game stalls and friendships strain. You need a ruling you can trust—instantly.

Court-side scenario
Doubles match, ad-court. Your partner is serving and the return team claims a foot fault—the server's foot touched the baseline before contact. Your partner disagrees. The other team wants the point replayed; your partner says it counts. Nobody budges. The Tennis Official settles it in seconds.
